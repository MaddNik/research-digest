Fault-tolerant quantum computing requires implementation of arbitrary phase rotations, yet approximation methods introduce accumulated errors. This work introduces a surface-code cultivation protocol for creating reusable logical catalyst states that implement exact fine dyadic phase gates Z raised to the power of 2 raised to the power of negative b through phase kickback. The approach eliminates approximation errors from synthesis and achieves constant-depth online gate implementation. The researchers demonstrate this with a concrete square-root T gate construction using nine-qubit circuits and show that at 10 raised to the power of negative 3 physical error rates, the postselected catalyst reaches distance-seven code blocks with approximately 10 raised to the power of negative 6 logical leakage rates. This work addresses a key challenge in fault-tolerant quantum computing by enabling exact, reusable logical magic states for precise phase manipulation without synthesis errors.
