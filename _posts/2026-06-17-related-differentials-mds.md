A pair of differences is a related differential for a linear layer M if, for every
input and output coordinate, at least one of the two values vanishes or the two
values coincide; such related differentials underlie the zero-difference attack on
AES of Bardeh and Rijmen. Daemen and Rijmen raised the question of which maximum
distance separable (MDS) matrices admit them, showing that every 4-by-4 circulant
MDS matrix does while some Hadamard ones do not. Building on the authors' earlier
characterization of the 3-by-3 case by fifteen explicit equations, this paper
settles the 4-by-4 case completely: an MDS matrix over a binary extension field
admits a related differential if and only if at least one of 280 explicit polynomial
equations in the nine free entries of its reduced form holds. The equations comprise
70 quadratic and 210 cubic forms that are pairwise distinct, irreducible, and
coprime, and they fall into 27 orbits under natural symmetries. The authors also
determine the deeper structure of this equation set, including that the 280
equations span a 14-dimensional binary space and satisfy exactly 560 additive
relations.
