---

The authors use the strong second-order optical nonlinearity of thin-film lithium niobate to generate photon pairs through spontaneous parametric down-conversion inside a racetrack microresonator cavity. The device produces correlated pairs across 80 distinct frequency channels in the C and L optical bands, which the authors describe as the highest channel count reported for this type of source. Measured performance includes a pair generation rate of approximately 126 kilohertz per microwatt of pump power. Heralded single-photon operation is confirmed with a measured second-order autocorrelation dip of 0.0544. The authors position the result as advancing multiplexed quantum light sources on nanophotonic platforms.
