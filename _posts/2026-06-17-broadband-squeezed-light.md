description: "Waveguide optical parametric amplifiers with external dispersion compensation deliver broadband squeezed light beyond 4 terahertz of offset."
---

The authors demonstrate broadband phase-sensitive amplification of squeezed light
generated by waveguide optical parametric amplifiers paired with external
dispersion compensation. They address how group velocity dispersion limits the
observable squeezing bandwidth and introduce compensation between two amplifiers to
overcome it. They report a maximum squeezing of 5.9 decibels near the carrier
frequency and more than 5 decibels of squeezing up to a frequency offset of 4.5
terahertz from the carrier. This broadband squeezing is relevant to high-speed
continuous-variable quantum information processing and measurement.
