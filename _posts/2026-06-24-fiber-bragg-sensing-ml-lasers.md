Distributed fiber sensing requires precise alignment of narrow-linewidth lasers with fiber Bragg grating resonances, but manual tuning becomes impractical at scale. This work presents an intelligent sensing system combining machine learning with hybrid III-V/SiN tunable lasers achieving unprecedented sub-picometer wavelength control and mode-hop-free operation. A gradient-descent algorithm enables continuous tuning with less than 0.1 picometer resolution and less than 0.5 decibel power variation, allowing precise alignment for acoustic detection. A demonstration with four lasers monitored 16 sensors across metallic structures over a 35 nanometer wavelength range, with the system autonomously identifying sensor resonances and dynamically tracking spectral shifts in response to acoustic events. The approach combines narrow linewidth (less than 10 kilohertz) with wide tunability to enable robust, scalable, and high-resolution sensing suitable for distributed structural health monitoring networks in critical infrastructure applications.
