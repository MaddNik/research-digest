Degenerate optical parametric oscillations in microresonators are valuable for frequency conversion and quantum light generation, but high pump power thresholds limit practical implementation. This work studies the excitation of degenerate optical parametric oscillations via bichromatic pumping in normal-dispersion photonic-crystal microresonators through numerical simulations. The photonic-crystal structure with two split modes placed symmetrically at a particular interval from the pumped modes provides significant reduction in pump power threshold for the degenerate process. The researchers determined the parameter range for this threshold reduction phenomenon and demonstrated that introducing mode splitting at the signal mode located between the pumped modes actually increases the generation threshold. These findings enable engineering of photonic-crystal designs to facilitate low-threshold parametric amplification and oscillation for practical applications.
