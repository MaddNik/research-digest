The author presents NanoPhotoNet-Lase, a physics-informed neural network framework that embeds electromagnetic and laser rate equations directly into training to accelerate the design of active metasurface nanolasers. By coupling the vector Helmholtz equation with four-level population dynamics of a dye gain medium, the model predicts optical responses and estimates lasing thresholds across many nanostructure geometries and material choices. Using a high-index metasurface cavity, the model identifies geometries supporting quasi-bound states in the continuum with strong field confinement and high quality factors. An experimental nanolaser built with Rhodamine B dye gain matched the model's predicted lasing threshold of 565 microjoules per square centimeter and 620 nanometer emission wavelength to within 1 percent. The framework was further used to design phase-gradient nanolaser metalenses producing coherent, directional, focused, or steered emission, demonstrating a machine-learning-guided route to programmable coherent light sources on a chip.
