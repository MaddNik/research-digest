The authors propose a photonic platform for simulating arbitrary single-qubit open-system dynamics using a single photon traveling through an open Mach-Zehnder interferometer. A birefringent quartz plate couples the photon's polarization to its frequency, and by treating the frequency degree of freedom as an effective environment, the authors analytically derive the resulting reduced dynamics of the polarization qubit. Adjusting the photon's frequency distribution and interferometric parameters lets the polarization state's population and coherence evolve in a controllable, tunable way instead of the usual pure dephasing produced by a quartz plate alone. The authors benchmark the simulator against standard open-system evolutions, including depolarization and non-Markovian dynamics, and report high accuracy. They describe this polarization-frequency engineered interferometer as a versatile, experimentally accessible protocol for simulating open quantum systems.
