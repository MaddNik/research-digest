description: "A two-dimensional discrete complex Ginzburg-Landau model of exciton-polariton lattices shows that reservoir computing performance peaks near the edge of dynamical stability."
---

The authors model driven-dissipative exciton-polariton lattices as a two-dimensional discrete complex Ginzburg-Landau system and use it as a physical reservoir computer. They find that classification performance is optimal near the edge of stability, the regime where the maximum Lyapunov exponent approaches zero from below. On a 52-class handwritten letter recognition task, the system operating near this edge substantially outperforms a linear baseline. The authors show this edge-of-stability principle holds consistently across a range of control parameters, and they discuss feasibility of implementing the approach on edge computing hardware. The work is offered as a general design guideline for physical reservoir computing systems built from nonlinear photonic lattices.
