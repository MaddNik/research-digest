The authors apply temporal unitary transforms to generate 16-QAM optical signals
at symbol rates up to 220 gigabaud while using only 50 gigahertz of electrical
bandwidth. The method is theoretically lossless and enables generation of
arbitrary optical waveforms that exceed the bandwidth limitations of individual
modulators. This addresses a core constraint in high-speed coherent optical
interconnects, where electrical driver and modulator bandwidth caps the achievable
symbol rate. The paper has been accepted for the 2026 European Conference on
Optical Communications.
