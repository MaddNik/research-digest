byline: "Schittenhelm et al. (arXiv:2607.01477, Wed 01 Jul 2026)"
description: "A silicon-on-insulator meta-grating coupler emits large, near diffraction-limited collimated beams directly from a photonic chip."
---

Photonic integrated circuits on silicon-on-insulator typically use grating couplers to interface with free space, but scaling these couplers to collimated beam diameters beyond 100 micrometers requires very weak, spatially distributed coupling whose resulting beam quality had not been well characterized before this work. The authors design a meta-grating coupler by spatially tailoring the coupling strength through a sub-wavelength unit cell, independently setting the local emission angle to synthesize a target emission profile. The resulting device emits a collimated, near-Gaussian beam with a waist diameter of about 300 micrometers and a measured beam quality factor M squared of 1.10 or better. The authors state the design approach extends to other target beam profiles, such as flat-top or higher-order modes. This makes meta-grating couplers a practical chip-to-free-space interface for applications sensitive to mode matching, such as coupling light into high-finesse resonators or trapping cold atoms.
