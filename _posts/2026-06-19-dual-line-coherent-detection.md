---

The authors experimentally demonstrate a coherent detection method that uses an optical
frequency comb as the local oscillator. The approach achieves 200 gigahertz of offset
tolerance for 400 gigabit-per-second signals with low penalty. This relaxed
frequency-offset requirement allows coherent transceivers to be uncooled and
cost-effective. The method also requires minimal additional digital signal processing to
handle large frequency deviations. The authors frame the technique as a practical path
toward simplified, low-cost coherent links.
