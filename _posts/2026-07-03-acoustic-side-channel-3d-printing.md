This paper demonstrates a side-channel intellectual-property theft attack against additive manufacturing systems. Using a pair of ordinary smartphones positioned 60 centimeters from the target printer in a non-line-of-sight configuration, the researchers captured combined acoustic and magnetic emissions produced during printing. From these multi-modal recordings they reconstructed the underlying G-code commands, the low-level instructions that define an object's geometry and printing process, achieving a 98.89 percent command-level reconstruction accuracy. The authors further tested how well the attack transfers across different 3D printer models and physical environments. The results highlight that manufacturing intellectual property can leak through commodity consumer devices without any physical access to the machine, and the authors argue for side-channel-aware mitigations in additive manufacturing systems.
