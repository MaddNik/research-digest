The authors experimentally investigate error suppression across fiber-connected superconducting quantum processing units operating under realistic noise conditions, a setting relevant to future distributed or networked quantum data centers. By layering multiple error mitigation techniques together, they measure the resulting gains in operational fidelity relative to using any single technique alone. The reported result is an improvement of over 20 percent in operational fidelity across the interconnected processing units. The work provides an experimental benchmark for how layered error mitigation strategies perform in a realistic fiber-interconnected multi-processor setting, relevant to scaling quantum computing systems that rely on optical links between distributed processing units.
