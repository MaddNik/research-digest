The paper introduces a framework called Hierarchical Identity-Based Signature with
Designated Aggregator (HIBS-DA), in which users at different levels of an
organizational hierarchy produce individual signatures that a designated aggregator
can combine into a single compact signature. The authors present what they describe
as the first lattice-based construction of this primitive, placing it in the
post-quantum setting. The design is aimed at reducing communication and
verification costs in large-scale environments such as universities, corporations,
and government agencies. The scheme is presented with arguments for correctness,
security, and resistance to forgery. The work is categorized under cryptography and
security as well as information theory.
