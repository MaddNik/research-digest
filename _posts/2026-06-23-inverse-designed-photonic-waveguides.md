Photonic crystal waveguides enable sub-wavelength light confinement and manipulation of propagation mode group velocity, enhancing light-matter interactions for integrated optical technologies. This work combines inverse design with computational optimization techniques to dramatically improve PCW performance across multiple dimensions. By systematically exploring parameter trade-offs through computational methods, the researchers achieve up to an order of magnitude increase in bandwidth and up to 4× reduction in propagation loss compared to conventional designs, while reducing optimization time by 100×. The methodology is demonstrated on two applications: broadband, position-tolerant Purcell enhancement for quantum emitters, and compact phase shifters for optical communications, each showing significant improvements over existing designs. The general optimization framework enables systematic exploration of device parameter spaces that would be infeasible through manual iteration, opening new directions for high-performance integrated photonic technologies.
