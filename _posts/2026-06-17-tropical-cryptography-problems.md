description: "A survey of algebraic and computational problems arising from the design and cryptanalysis of tropical cryptography protocols."

Tropical cryptography, proposed by Grigoriev and Shpilrain in 2011, uses tropical
algebraic structures to build protocols, and recent years have seen numerous such
protocols introduced alongside many attacks on them. This paper gives an overview of
the purely algebraic and computational problems that have emerged from both
designing secure schemes and analyzing their vulnerabilities. The authors discuss
the complexity of solving certain classes of systems of equations over tropical and
similar structures, together with algorithms and approaches for solving them, and
present results on the asymptotic density of satisfiable systems of equations of
special forms over tropical algebras. They also treat the discrete logarithm
problem, the two-sided discrete logarithm problem, the knapsack problem, and the
subset sum problem over tropical matrix structures. A generalization of marginal
sets for tropical semirings and semigroups is considered, as well as different
classes of pairwise commuting matrices. The work consolidates open questions for
this line of research.
