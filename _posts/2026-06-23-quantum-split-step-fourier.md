Modeling quantum effects in nonlinear optical systems requires accurate tracking of how quantum fluctuations evolve and become entangled across spectral components. This work introduces the Quantum Split-Step Fourier algorithm, which extends traditional split-step propagation of the nonlinear Schrödinger equation by incorporating commutator-preserving Bogoliubov evolution for Gaussian quantum fluctuations. The method enables systematic analysis of how selected radiation bands develop increasing entropy and lose purity through entanglement with other spectral components. Notably, despite radiation occupying many frequency bins, the quantum state exhibits surprisingly pronounced low-dimensional structure dominated by only a few Williamson modes, dramatically reducing computational complexity. The algorithm provides diagnostic capabilities for studying quantum correlations in frequency conversion, supercontinuum generation, and squeezed-light formation within waveguide systems, enabling practical simulation of quantum light generation in nonlinear integrated photonics.
