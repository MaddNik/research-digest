---

This work demonstrates an optoelectronic Ising machine with 64 all-to-all
connected spins using pulsed time-division multiplexing, integrating coupling and
nonlinear mechanisms in a 65 nm CMOS chip (3.1 square mm active area) with the
loop closed via a compact optical fiber. Operating at 1 GHz with 4-bit coupling
weights, it is benchmarked on 3-SAT and MaxCut, reaching time- and
energy-to-solution as low as 7.4 microseconds and 2.9 microjoules. The authors
report nanosecond annealing times and claim at least a three-order-of-magnitude
improvement over prior all-to-all connected works.
