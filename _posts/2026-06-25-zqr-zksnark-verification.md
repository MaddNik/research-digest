Privacy-preserving technologies like zero-knowledge proofs offer strong privacy guarantees but are often hindered by technical complexity, limiting practical adoption in daily applications. This work presents a framework integrating QR codes with blockchain and large-language models to make zero-knowledge proof verification accessible on mobile devices. The approach bridges the gap between theoretical privacy primitives and practical usability by encoding proofs in QR format for easy mobile scanning and processing. The researchers evaluated both temporal and financial costs of the framework, demonstrating feasibility for compact proof representation on mobile platforms. By lowering the technical barrier to privacy-preserving verification, this work addresses a critical adoption bottleneck. The framework discusses applications across various privacy-sensitive domains and future directions for broader deployment of privacy-preserving technologies in mainstream consumer applications.
