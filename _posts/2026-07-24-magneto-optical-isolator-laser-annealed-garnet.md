description: "A silicon waveguide isolator is built by locally laser annealing cerium substituted yttrium iron garnet directly on chip instead of using furnace annealing."
---

The authors integrate a magneto-optical thin film onto a silicon Mach-Zehnder interferometer using local laser annealing performed in vacuum, which crystallizes the garnet film without thermally damaging nearby silicon structures or metal electrodes. This sidesteps the incompatibility between the high annealing temperatures magneto-optical garnets need and the heat sensitivity of silicon photonic circuits. The resulting device achieves an isolation ratio of 13.6 decibels at 1540 nanometers and a Faraday rotation of 0.092 degrees per micrometer. The authors present this as a high-throughput, mass-production-compatible route to monolithic optical isolators on silicon.
