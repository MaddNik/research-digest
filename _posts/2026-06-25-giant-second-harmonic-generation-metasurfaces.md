Two-dimensional materials like 3R-phase molybdenum disulfide offer strong second-order optical nonlinearities in an atomically thin platform, yet second-harmonic generation efficiency remains fundamentally limited by weak light-matter interaction and poor phase matching. This work introduces NanoPhotoNet-PINL, a physics-informed AI-driven inverse design framework based on hybrid convolutional and deep neural network autoencoders tailored for nonlinear multi-layer metasurfaces. The model maps target dual-resonant reflection spectra at fundamental and second-harmonic wavelengths to the required multi-layer geometries and compositions that maximize effective nonlinear overlap with embedded 3R-MoS2. By integrating Maxwell-based nonlinear electrodynamics into the inverse design loop, the framework computes second-harmonic conversion efficiency and modal overlap factors for physics-guided training. The optimized dual-resonant metasurfaces achieve more than three orders of magnitude enhancement in second-harmonic generation intensity compared to bare 3R-MoS2 on planar substrate, establishing a generalizable paradigm for intelligent inverse design of nonlinear metasurfaces.
