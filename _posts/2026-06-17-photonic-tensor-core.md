---

The authors present a photonic tensor core on a thin-film lithium niobate
platform that pairs a homodyne photonic matrix with a bus-readout
time-integrating array. The architecture reduces the high-speed DAC and
electro-optic interconversion overhead for matrix operations from O(n squared) to
O(n), and uses orthogonal horizontal and vertical crossbars to route data and
weights independently, avoiding beam-combining loss while allowing synchronous
updates of both. They frame it as a hardware-efficient foundation for
high-bandwidth photonic AI processors.
