---

The paper studies the Al1 center, a point defect in silicon, embedded in isotopically purified nanophotonic waveguide devices. The emitter shows high-purity single-photon emission and an excited-state lifetime substantially shorter than comparable benchmark defect systems. The authors also report a narrow homogeneous linewidth and demonstrate spin-selective optical pumping of the ground-state spin. These properties position the Al1 center as a telecom-band spin-photon interface compatible with silicon photonic integration for quantum network applications.
