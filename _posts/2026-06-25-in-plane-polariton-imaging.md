Polariton interferometry through optical near-field microscopy has become essential for studying strongly confined evanescent modes in nanophotonics, yet traditional scattering-type approaches mostly access out-of-plane field components owing to nanotip geometry. This work demonstrates a complementary far-field approach using nonlinear infrared-visible sum-frequency generation microscopy, achieving high spatial resolution through the short visible wavelength of the nonlinear signal while remaining sensitive to evanescent modes in the infrared. The symmetry selection rules of the sum-frequency process enable polarization-selective imaging that directly measures both in-plane polariton field components through spatial interferometry. Experimental demonstration at the AlN-air interface with surface phonon polaritons shows that the method captures peculiar propagation patterns reproducible by semi-analytical modeling, and hyperspectral imaging with tunable narrowband lasers further reveals the polariton dispersion. The wide-field methodology promises high-throughput, in-depth studies of infrared nanophotonic structures for applications including hyperbolic polaritons and skyrmion characterization.
