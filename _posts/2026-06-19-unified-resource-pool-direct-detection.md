The work targets scaling optical communication capacity without inflating receiver
complexity by treating wavelength, polarization, and intensity as one unified symbol
space rather than separate dimensions. An integrated disordered photonic processor
transforms incoming optical states into distinguishable electrical fingerprints that
can be recovered by direct detection. The authors report recovery of 4096 composite
symbols, corresponding to 12 bits per symbol slot, with a bit error rate of 4.25 times
10 to the minus 4 after 10 kilometers of standard fiber transmission. They further show
the scheme works with dense polarization alphabets, wavelength-indexed state expansion,
and high-power operation over hollow-core fiber. The authors position disorder-enabled
joint projection as a hardware-efficient alternative to conventional dimension-separated
receiver designs.
