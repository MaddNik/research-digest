The work fabricates and characterizes a diamond-on-alumina dual-taper waveguide structure
that incorporates tin-vacancy centers, which are promising solid-state quantum bits for
integrated quantum photonics. The authors demonstrate optical coupling between the diamond
nanobeam and the underlying alumina waveguide. They use a bilayer fabrication method that
is compatible with wafer-scale lithography. Across all optically active devices, they
observe clear guided emission from the negatively charged tin-vacancy centers, confirming
effective optical coupling in the integrated structure. The authors present this as a
scalable fabrication route toward integrating diamond color centers with photonic
waveguides.
