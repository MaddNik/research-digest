---

The authors introduce a wavelength-addressable diffractive optical network that
turns illumination wavelength into a high-dimensional control parameter for
programmable two-dimensional beam steering. The passive architecture uses
cascaded, spatially optimized diffractive layers designed via deep learning to map
distinct wavelengths to specific output angles. Unlike conventional single-layer
dispersive elements, the framework enables nonlinear wavefront transformations
without mechanical scanning or electronic phase control. The team demonstrates
wavelength-controlled steering across 625 channels spanning 400 to 750 nanometers,
achieving a 25 by 25 addressable beam position array. Validation was performed
experimentally in both terahertz and visible spectral regimes.
