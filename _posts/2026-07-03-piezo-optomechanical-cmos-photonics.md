---

The authors built a wafer-scale process that bonds photonic integrated circuits containing piezo-optomechanical devices onto completed CMOS driver wafers, forming a high-density digital backplane with more than 2 million electrical connections per die. In this system, photonic components act as digital-to-analog converters, translating low-voltage digital electronic signals into multi-bit analog optical phase and amplitude modulation. The platform supports parallel control of many optical components through a standard HDMI interface. The team reports wafer-scale integration of these devices on an electronic backplane with consistent uniformity and yield validated across multiple reticles. This establishes a manufacturing-compatible route for combining electronic control circuitry with optomechanical photonic devices at scale, on 200 millimeter wafers.
