The paper addresses thermal instabilities that hinder chip-based nonlinear photonics by
demonstrating deterministic Kerr soliton comb generation in compact microresonators with
line spacings as low as 16 gigahertz. The authors develop an experimentally validated
thermal model of the device behavior. They implement fast feedback control to stabilize
cavity-soliton states that would otherwise be unstable. This combination enables thermally
stable and highly compact soliton microcomb devices. The authors note applicability across
photonic platforms for optical communications, precision metrology, microwave generation,
and LIDAR.
