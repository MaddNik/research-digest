Gaussian beams and Helmholtz waves represent complementary descriptions of optical propagation, yet the mathematical connection between these frameworks has remained opaque. This work identifies the scale ratio between the Helmholtz transverse-wavenumber scale and the paraxial Gaussian-beam-width scale as the physical parameter connecting separable Gaussian beams with Helmholtz waves. The optical scale ratio factors the Helmholtz-to-paraxial angular-spectrum restriction with the paraxial-to-Helmholtz large-Rayleigh-range limit, revealing how algebraic Inönü-Wigner contraction and Cayley-Klein deformation govern optical limits. The spectral contraction connects entire families of beams and waves: Hermite and Laguerre Gaussian beam families correspond with plane and Bessel wave families, while Ince and Boyer-Wolf Gaussian families relate to Mathieu and Weber wave families, respectively. This framework establishes connections at the coordinate, differential equation, algebraic deformation, and separation operator levels, providing systematic understanding of the relationship between paraxial and full Helmholtz descriptions.
