description: "Integrated thin-film lithium tantalate transducers coherently convert C-band optical photons to microwave photons with multi-day stability and low added noise."
---

The paper demonstrates integrated electro-optic microwave-to-optical transducers built
on thin-film lithium tantalate for quantum applications. Superconducting microwave
resonators are coupled to tunable photonic-molecule optical resonators, achieving
coherent bidirectional conversion between C-band optical photons and microwave photons
in the 4.9 to 5.5 gigahertz range. The authors report on-chip efficiencies consistent
with theory, continuous multi-day operation with minimal feedback using a static bias
field, and added noise of less than one photon for 100 microsecond pulses. They argue
the platform addresses bias drift, efficiency, and fabrication limitations seen in
competing lithium niobate approaches. The result is presented as a scalable route toward
quantum interconnects and modular quantum processors.
