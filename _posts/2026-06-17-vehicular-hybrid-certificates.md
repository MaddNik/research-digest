byline: "Abel C. H. Chen et al. (arXiv:2606.14008, Jun 12 2026)"
description: "A hybrid elliptic-curve plus post-quantum certificate and pseudonym scheme for standardized vehicular communication credential systems."
---

The paper observes that IEEE and ETSI vehicular communication standards, including
the Security Credential Management System and Butterfly Key Expansion, rely mainly
on elliptic-curve cryptography that may be vulnerable to future quantum attacks. To
address this, the authors propose a hybrid certificate that combines elliptic-curve
cryptography with post-quantum cryptography so that infrastructure can rest on more
quantum-resilient foundations. They present a generalized pseudonym scheme
compatible with multiple cryptographic algorithms and designed so that no
correlation can be inferred between the public key in a pseudonym certificate and
that in an enrollment certificate. The study includes a performance evaluation of
RSA, elliptic-curve cryptography, and NIST-standardized post-quantum algorithms,
comparing message length and computation time. Based on the results, the authors
recommend pseudonym schemes using hybrid certificates suited for secure and
efficient vehicular use.
