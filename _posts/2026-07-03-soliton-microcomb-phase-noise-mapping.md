description: "A simulation study traces how individual noise sources, including Raman nonlinearity, combine to set the phase-noise floor of soliton microcombs."
---

Phase noise limits the coherence and stability of soliton microcombs used in integrated photonic frequency synthesis, but its physical origin is hard to isolate because several noise sources act at once. The authors combine subspace tracking with multi-source Ikeda-map simulations, switching individual noise sources and the Raman nonlinearity on and off to trace each one's contribution to common-mode and repetition-rate comb noise. Without Raman scattering, pump phase noise stays purely common-mode while shot noise and amplified spontaneous emission drive repetition-rate noise. With Raman scattering included, the nonlinearity converts pump phase noise into repetition-rate noise, producing a parabolic linewidth profile with a quiet point below the pump linewidth. When all noise sources are present together, shot noise, amplified spontaneous emission, and relative intensity noise raise the noise floor and shift the quiet point toward the pump, leading the authors to conclude that intracavity dynamics actively partition noise among comb lines rather than simply carrying it.
