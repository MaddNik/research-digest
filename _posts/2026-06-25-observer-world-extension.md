Impagliazzo's five worlds provide a foundational classification of computational assumptions along the existence of cryptographic primitives, yet all five worlds implicitly assume every party, including adversaries, observes the full input. This work makes this assumption explicit and relaxes it by introducing a second orthogonal axis: the observational axis, defined by an observer hierarchy. Relaxing the observational assumption reveals structural phenomena, such as the collapse P subscript observer subscript profiler equals NP subscript observer subscript profiler, that the original five-world framework cannot express. The researchers prove that this collapse holds unconditionally in all five worlds, showing that observational blindness and computational hardness are independent dimensions. The work defines the Observer World framework, classifies all world-observer pairs, identifies labeled cells, and introduces a parametric family modeling partial violations of observational invariants. The framework also interfaces with physical information limits, including thermodynamic, quantum, and cosmological bounds, extending foundational complexity-theoretic understanding.
