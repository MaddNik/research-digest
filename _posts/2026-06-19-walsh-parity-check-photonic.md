The authors implement two nested Walsh parity-check filters on Quandela's Belenos cloud
photonic processor, using single-photon spatial modes indexed by the vertices of a cube.
The filters realize the classical eight-comma-seven-comma-two single-parity-check code
and the eight-comma-four-comma-four extended Hamming code. Rather than demonstrating
error correction, the experiment verifies leakage suppression and syndrome routing using
postselected single-photon detections across more than 340,000 events. Neutral inputs
achieved DC-port leakage between 0.02 percent and 1.1 percent, about 21 times suppression
relative to the ideal baseline, while the three face-parity syndrome channels routed to
the predicted ports with 94 to 99 percent selectivity. The authors also quantify
calibration offsets and compilation systematics and report a Hong-Ou-Mandel degradation
episode that recovered after recalibration.
