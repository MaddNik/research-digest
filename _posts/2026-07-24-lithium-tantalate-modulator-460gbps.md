The authors fabricate thin-film lithium tantalate electro-optic modulators using a technique that undercuts the silicon handle wafer beneath the traveling-wave electrodes. This isolation reduces microwave losses and removes parasitic surface conductance that otherwise limits high-frequency performance. The resulting 8 millimeter long device achieves a 3 decibel electro-optic bandwidth of 110 gigahertz with a half-wave voltage of 5.1 volts. Using PAM8 signaling the modulator supports a net data rate of 460 gigabits per second, which the authors present as a practical route to unlocking the full bandwidth potential of lithium tantalate on silicon.
