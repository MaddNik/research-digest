Deterministic single-photon sources are a long-standing bottleneck for linear optical quantum computing and quantum communication. The authors propose a scheme based on a Delta-type three-level atom coupled to a single-mode cavity and driven by two classical fields, applicable in both strong and ultrastrong atom-cavity coupling regimes. Under continuous-wave driving in the strong coupling regime, the scheme predicts a normalized second-order correlation function around one part in a million, photon indistinguishability of 98.73 percent, and state purity of 99.95 percent. The ultrastrong coupling regime further suppresses multi-photon events to about one part in one hundred million while raising indistinguishability and purity. Under pulsed driving in the ultrastrong coupling regime, the scheme predicts emission efficiency, indistinguishability, and purity all above 98 percent under resonant conditions, with somewhat lower indistinguishability under detuned conditions, offering a theoretical route toward higher-quality deterministic single-photon devices.
