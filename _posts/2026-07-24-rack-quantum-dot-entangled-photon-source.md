The authors describe a rack-mounted quantum light source built around a semiconductor quantum dot emitting at telecom C-band wavelengths, combined with a tunable pulsed laser, filtering optics, and fiber coupling in one enclosure. The system achieves above 50 percent transmission for both exciton and biexciton photons and reports a record coincidence rate for entangled photon pair emission in this wavelength band. The authors argue this level of packaging and performance demonstrates that quantum dot single and entangled photon sources can be deployed within existing telecommunications infrastructure. They frame the result as a step toward practical quantum internet applications.
