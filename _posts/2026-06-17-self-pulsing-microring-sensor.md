---

The authors demonstrate that self-pulsing dynamics in a microring resonator
network can process time-dependent signals from optical sensors. The approach
reduces the minimum sampling rate by at least one order of magnitude relative to
conventional acquisition. It combines fiber sensing measurements taken at
different perturbation locations and frequencies with microring resonator network
measurements at multiple output ports, input power levels, and laser wavelengths.
The result points toward bandwidth-efficient event detection using nonlinear
integrated photonic dynamics as a physical computing layer for sensing.
