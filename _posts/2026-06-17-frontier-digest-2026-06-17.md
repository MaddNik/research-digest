## 📡 Photonics

### [Spatiotemporally Interleaved Homodyne Photonic Tensor Core](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16150)
*Yun-Long Nie, Hang Song, De-Hui Huang, et al. · arXiv:2606.16150 · Jun 15, 2026*

The authors present a photonic tensor core on a thin-film lithium niobate platform
that pairs a homodyne photonic matrix with a bus-readout time-integrating array.
The architecture reduces the high-speed DAC and electro-optic interconversion
overhead for matrix operations from O(n²) to O(n), and uses orthogonal
horizontal/vertical crossbars to route data and weights independently, avoiding
beam-combining loss while allowing synchronous updates of both. They frame it as a
hardware-efficient foundation for high-bandwidth photonic AI processors.

### [A Scalable All-to-All Reconfigurable Ising Solver Using Pulsed Time-Division Multiplexing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16929)
*Henry Love, Zhehao Yu, Mohammad Alimadadi, et al. · arXiv:2606.16929 · Jun 15, 2026*

This work demonstrates an optoelectronic Ising machine with 64 all-to-all connected
spins using pulsed time-division multiplexing, integrating coupling and nonlinear
mechanisms in a 65 nm CMOS chip (3.1 mm² active area) with the loop closed via a
compact optical fiber. Operating at 1 GHz with 4-bit coupling weights, it is
benchmarked on 3-SAT and MaxCut, reaching time- and energy-to-solution as low as
7.4 µs and 2.9 µJ. The authors report nanosecond annealing times and claim at least
a three-order-of-magnitude improvement over prior all-to-all connected works.

### [Octave bandwidth 3D-Printed Couplers for Low-Loss Thin-Film Lithium Tantalate Circuits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16398)
*Erik Jung, Xinyu Ma, Jan Brandes, et al. · arXiv:2606.16398 · Jun 15, 2026*

The paper reports out-of-plane polymer coupling interfaces fabricated by 3D direct
laser writing for lithium tantalate on insulator (LTOI) waveguides, achieving
coupling losses of 0.9 dB (strip) and 1.25 dB (rib) per interface. The couplers show
a 3 dB bandwidth spanning more than an octave (850–1740 nm) and stable operation at
1 W input, with on-chip propagation loss below 0.1 dB/cm. The octave span lets the
platform interface both fundamental and second-harmonic signals, which the authors
note is attractive for SHG-based quantum squeezing.

### [Integrated tunable mid-infrared electro-optic frequency comb generator based on nonlinear conversion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16324)
*Pierre Didier, Prakhar Jain, Tristan Kuttner, Oliver Pitz, Rachel Grange · arXiv:2606.16324 · Jun 15, 2026*

This work demonstrates a mid-infrared electro-optic frequency comb in thin-film
lithium niobate, combining an amplitude-modulated near-infrared pump with a
telecom-band EO comb (double-pass phase modulation) and difference-frequency
generation in a periodically poled waveguide. Tuning the seed laser and chip
temperature yields ~6 nm combs with >200 nm center-wavelength tunability and
RF-controlled comb spacing, reaching wavelengths up to 3.7 µm. The authors claim
this is the first integrated mid-IR EO comb source with independent electronic
control of both center wavelength and comb spacing.

### [Edge-Enhanced Diffractive Neural Networks Based on Spin-Multiplexed Nonlocal Metasurfaces](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16938)
*Qianqian He, Kenan Guo, Jumin Qiu, Shuyuan Xiao, Tingting Liu · arXiv:2606.16938 · Jun 15, 2026*

The authors propose an integrated nonlocal meta-platform that combines all-optical
edge detection and diffractive-neural-network classification via spin-multiplexing:

crescent-shaped nanopillars with about 55% polarization conversion efficiency, the
edge-enhanced single-layer network raises MNIST classification accuracy from 64.2%

### [Kerr Soliton Generation in Ultra-Compact Photonic Devices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18067)
*Garrett J. Beals, Yun Zhao, Sai Kanth Dacha, et al. · arXiv:2606.18067 · Jun 16, 2026*

The authors demonstrate deterministic, highly stable Kerr soliton comb generation in

## 🔐 Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)

### [Hierarchical Identity-Based Signature with Designated Aggregator from Lattices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14090)
*Stuti Kumari, Kunal Dey, Vikas Srivastava, Sumit Kumar Debnath · arXiv:2606.14090 · Jun 12, 2026*

The authors present what they describe as the first lattice-based construction of a
hierarchical identity-based signature scheme with a designated aggregator. The scheme
lets users at different hierarchical levels produce individual signatures that can be
aggregated into a single compact signature, reducing computational and communication
overhead. They argue it is secure against forgery and target large hierarchical
organizations such as universities, enterprises, and government agencies.

### [Measurement Study of Post-Quantum Readiness of Internet: 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16473)
*Vanishka Mohan Dubey, Gaurav Varshney · arXiv:2606.16473 · Jun 15, 2026*

This Internet-scale measurement examines PQC deployment across 32,011 domains. It
reports that roughly 49.3% of domains support hybrid post-quantum key exchange while
50.7% still use only classical key exchange, and finds 0% adoption of hybrid
post-quantum certificates, indicating authentication remains unprotected. It also
notes that 15.70% of domains, concentrated in banking and government, still rely on
TLS 1.2, and stresses that resilience against Harvest-Now-Decrypt-Later attacks needs
coordinated upgrades to both key exchange and certificate infrastructure.

### [Quantifying quantum risk: a measure of crypto agility](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17116)
*Coryan Wilson-Shah · arXiv:2606.17116 · Jun 15, 2026*

The paper introduces "rotation time" as a metric for cryptographic agility and
derives an approximation linking rotation-time tolerance to security-risk tolerance.
Using historical vulnerability data, it estimates rotation-time tolerances on the
order of hours to days, and argues that combining crypto agility with hybrid
encryption is a viable path to quantum-resistant systems, albeit with demanding
technical and operational requirements.

## 🧮 Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)

### [WHET: Welding Homomorphic Encryption to Accelerator Architectures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11541)
*Jongmin Kim, Hyesung Ji, Wonseok Choi, Hyunah Yu, Jung Ho Ahn · arXiv:2606.11541 · Jun 10, 2026*

Arguing that conventional FHE constructions are poorly matched to accelerator
hardware, the authors propose memory-centric, architecture-aware optimizations —
including fine-grained coefficient-to-slot transformation, plaintext compression, and
intermediate modulus raising — together with hardware refinements. They report
1.38–8.74× per-area performance improvements over prior FHE accelerators and claim
the first sub-millisecond CKKS bootstrapping.

### [Latency-Aware, High-Throughput Homomorphic AES Evaluation with CKKS](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1209)
*Taeseong Kim, Jonghoo Lee, Taeyeong Noh, Jung Hee Cheon, Guillaume Hanrot · IACR ePrint 2026/1209 · Jun 10, 2026*

This paper revisits homomorphic AES evaluation, used in transciphering to convert
AES-encrypted data into FHE ciphertexts without exposing the secret key. The authors
observe that CKKS-based methods exploit massive SIMD parallelism for throughput by

settings, and present an approach aimed at achieving both interactive latency and

## 🔑 Cryptography

### [Top Gun: Degree Annihilation Attacks on Poseidon](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1254)
*Antonio Sanso, Giuseppe Vitto · IACR ePrint 2026/1254 · Jun 16, 2026*

The authors introduce "degree annihilation," an algebraic-cryptanalysis framework for
the Poseidon permutation widely used in zero-knowledge proof systems. Rather than
skipping rounds, it imposes algebraic constraints that force dominant degree terms to
vanish, yielding polynomial systems of lower effective degree, and combines naturally
with classical round-skipping. As a proof of concept they derive new CICO-2 attacks
on reduced-round Poseidon and argue this is a new direction for cryptanalysis of
arithmetization-oriented primitives.

### [Accountable Asynchronous Multi-Party Computation](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1245)
*Pierre Civit, Rachid Guerraoui · IACR ePrint 2026/1245 · Jun 11, 2026*

This paper presents the first accountable asynchronous MPC protocol that securely
evaluates any arithmetic circuit asynchronously computable by a trusted third party.
The motivation is that in non-synchronous networks, classic partition arguments show
no t-resilient protocol can ensure safety for many functionalities once corruptions
reach certain thresholds; building in accountability lets honest parties detect and
deter safety violations with publicly verifiable evidence implicating faulty parties.

### [Balanced Additive Randomized Encodings for Shuffle Differential Privacy](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1239)
*Yu Wei, Jaspal Singh, Adya Agrawal, Vassilis Zikas · IACR ePrint 2026/1239 · Jun 11, 2026*

The authors give a client-efficient one-round compiler from central differential
privacy to computational shuffle DP, improving on the additive randomized encodings
(ARE) of Halevi et al. (CRYPTO 2023), whose construction incurs prohibitive
worst-case client costs. They construct a new "balanced" ARE scheme in which all
clients share the computational burden almost equally, targeting resource-constrained
devices such as mobile phones, IoT sensors, and web browsers.

### [Private Information Retrieval: Share Conversions vs. Decoding Polynomials](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1211)
*Amos Beimel, Or Lasri · IACR ePrint 2026/1211 · Jun 10, 2026*

This work studies the power of share conversions in matching-vector-based
information-theoretic PIR, comparing them to the decoding polynomials used in modern
constructions. The main result shows that any k-party share conversion from Z_m to a
field of characteristic p (with p not dividing m) implies a k-sparse decoding
polynomial, so share conversion can improve the communication of Alon et al.'s
protocol by at most a constant factor. The authors also show that for m a product of
r distinct primes such a conversion requires k ≥ r+1, lower-bounding the number of
servers.

## 🛡️ Hardware Security

### [MIPSBLEED: Uncovering Microarchitectural Timing Leaks in Pervasive Embedded Processors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16372)
*Ahmed Najeeb, Billy Bob Brumley · arXiv:2606.16372 · Jun 15, 2026*

The authors show that Simultaneous Multithreading (SMT) on MIPS-based embedded
platforms creates cross-core timing channels, and introduce MIPSBLEED, a framework
that uncovers leakage in three shared microarchitectural components: the L1 data
cache, L1 instruction cache, and the execution engine. Using assembly-level probes and
quantitative leakage assessment, they demonstrate high-resolution timing attacks that
require no privileged access, culminating in a single-trace key-recovery attack
against a real elliptic-curve cryptographic toolkit.

---

### [Partitioned Tags, Shared Data: Reconciling Strict Cache Isolation with Write-Shared Coherence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12259)
*Kartik Ramkrishnan, Stephen McCamant, Antonia Zhai, Pen Chung Yew · arXiv:2606.12259 · Jun 10, 2026*

Cache partitioning is a strong structural defense against eviction-based cache side
channels, but write-shared coherence breaks under strict partitioning. The authors
present SCP (Secure and Coherent Partitioning), which partitions only the tags while
sharing a single data pool sized so that capacity-driven cross-partition eviction
cannot occur, combining strict eviction isolation with write-shared coherence. They
report mitigation of attacks like Prime+Probe and Flush+Reload at +0.3% IPC and +2.8%
LLC SRAM cost.

### [InjectV: Modeling Fault Injection Attacks in RISC-V Simulation Environment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12011)
*Niccolò Lentini, Giorgio Fardo, Stefano Di Carlo, Alessandro Savino · arXiv:2606.12011 · Jun 10, 2026*

InjectV is a gem5-based fault-injection framework for RISC-V that enables precise,
guided fault injection at security-critical execution points such as control-flow
decisions, counters, and comparisons, supporting transient faults in registers and
memory. It targets pre-silicon vulnerability assessment, where physical fault
experiments are costly and limited. On FISSC benchmarks including hardened VerifyPIN
variants, the authors report a 95.8% time saving over traditional fault-injection
approaches in identifying fault-injection points.

### [Side-Channel Attacks Bypass Protection in 3D Printers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13952)
*Eric Yocam, Varghese Vaidyan, Micah Flack, Gurcan Comert, Judith L. Mwakalonge · arXiv:2606.13952 · Jun 11, 2026*

The paper presents an empirical evaluation of a deployed Active Motor Noise
Cancellation (AMNC) countermeasure against acoustic side-channel IP theft in
commercial FDM 3D printers, using synchronized acoustic and vibration recordings from
two AMNC-equipped Bambu Lab printers across 12 object classes. AMNC fully neutralizes
the acoustic channel (accuracy at the 8.33% random baseline), but the untargeted
vibration channel still leaks: a full-sequence temporal model reaches about 61%
classification accuracy. The authors conclude AMNC is an acoustic-only defense,
leaving vibration, magnetic, and power channels untouched.

### [Bifrost: Hybrid TEE-FHE Inference for Privacy-Preserving Transformer and LLM Serving](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17421)
*Chenghao Chen, Kailun Qin, Xiaolin Zhang, Chi Zhang, Dawu Gu · arXiv:2606.17421 · Jun 16, 2026*

Bifrost addresses the confidentiality problem of cloud-hosted transformer/LLM
inference, where user prompts are exposed to the cloud software stack and accelerator
runtime. It combines CPU-side trusted execution environments with fully homomorphic
encryption on accelerators, provisioning secrets only to an attested CPU TEE while
keeping the accelerator, device memory, driver/runtime stack, and host software
outside the trusted computing base. The authors report latency reductions of 9.25×
to 53.4× across model sizes via selective encrypted execution rather than full FHE.

### [AttackonCTF: Defending Hardware Security Competition Benchmarks in the Age of LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15809)
*Mohamadreza Rostami, Nikhilesh Singh, Stephen Muttathil, et al. · arXiv:2606.15809 · Jun 14, 2026*

The authors show that LLM-based detectors threaten the validity of hardware security
competitions (e.g., HackTheSilicon) by exploiting diff-style syntactic comparison
rather than genuine security reasoning, reaching an 83% detection rate. They propose
what they describe as the first LLM-oriented, semantics-preserving obfuscation
framework for such benchmarks, applying human-readable transformations and controlled
diff-noise while preserving functionality. On HackTheSilicon, the framework reduces
LLM detection accuracy by 50% with 10% obfuscation and by 78.6% under complete
obfuscation.

### [Anywhere, Any-Stymie: Remote Activation of Trojan Malware on LiDAR with Modulated Signals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17562)
*R. Spencer Hallyburton, Miroslav Pajic · arXiv:2606.17562 · Jun 16, 2026*

The paper identifies an attack surface in which dormant malware embedded in the LiDAR
sensing pipeline stays inactive during normal operation and is externally triggered
post-deployment without hardware or network access at attack time. The authors embed
point-cloud-manipulation malware into LiDAR firmware (developed in a closed test
environment with vendor support) and use an optical, modulated-signal trigger to
activate it, demonstrating false-object injection and real-object suppression at up to
300 ft static and 35 mph drive-by. They conclude the work highlights the need for
stronger integrity guarantees across the LiDAR development and deployment pipeline.
